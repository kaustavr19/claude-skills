# Deep Lens — SaaS / B2B SEO

Run only when selected. For software products, B2B services, and platforms whose
SEO goal is qualified sign-ups/demos, not transactional product sales. The
playbook differs from e-commerce and from publisher content.

## Checks
- **Bottom-of-funnel intent pages:** does the site own its highest-intent
  commercial queries? The classic SaaS money pages:
  - **Comparison pages** ("X vs Y") — capture buyers evaluating alternatives.
  - **"Alternative to [competitor]" pages** — high commercial intent.
  - **Use-case / solution pages** — "[product] for [industry/role/job]".
  - **Pricing page** — often a top organic entry point; ensure it's indexable
    and not thin.
  Identify which exist, which are missing, and which competitors own.
- **Programmatic SEO (where appropriate):** templated pages generated at scale
  from structured data (integrations, templates, locations, "[tool] for [X]").
  Powerful for SaaS but risky — check for thin/duplicate doorway pages and
  ensure each programmatic page has genuine unique value and isn't bloating the
  index. If absent but the data model supports it, flag the opportunity.
- **Documentation & knowledge base indexing:** docs drive huge long-tail
  organic traffic and feed AI assistants. Are docs indexable, well-structured,
  and internally linked? Are they on a subdomain or subfolder (subfolder usually
  consolidates authority better)? Is help content `noindex`'d when it shouldn't
  be?
- **Product-led / top-of-funnel content:** educational content targeting the
  problems the product solves, linked down-funnel to solution pages. Check the
  cluster exists and connects to conversion paths.
- **SoftwareApplication / Product schema:** appropriate schema for the product,
  plus `Organization` and `FAQPage` where relevant.
- **Free tools / calculators as link magnets:** SaaS sites win links with free
  interactive tools. Identify linkable-asset opportunities (overlaps Off-page).
- **Trial/demo conversion paths:** are high-traffic organic pages routed to
  clear CTAs? SEO that doesn't convert to pipeline is vanity.
- **Trust & E-E-A-T for B2B:** case studies, customer logos, security/compliance
  pages, named expert authors — buyers and search engines both weigh these.

## GEO note
SaaS buyers increasingly research via AI assistants ("best tool for X",
"alternatives to Y"). Comparison pages, docs, and well-structured product facts
are highly citable — allow the answer/search crawlers (Pillar 6) and make
product facts explicit and well-structured.

## Reporting
Lead with missing bottom-of-funnel pages (comparison/alternative/use-case) and
docs indexability — usually the fastest pipeline-relevant wins. Treat
programmatic SEO as opportunity *and* risk; never recommend scale without a
unique-value bar.
