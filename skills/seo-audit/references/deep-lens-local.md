# Deep Lens — Local SEO

Run only when selected. For businesses with a physical location or a defined
service area (shops, restaurants, clinics, local services). Goal: win the local
pack, Google Maps, and "near me" / "in [city]" searches.

## Checks
- **Google Business Profile (GBP):** claimed, verified, complete? Correct
  primary + secondary categories, hours, photos, attributes, products/services.
  An unclaimed or thin GBP is usually the top local finding.
- **NAP consistency:** Name, Address, Phone identical across the website, GBP,
  and major directories. Inconsistency (old address, formatting differences)
  fragments local authority. Check the site's footer/contact page against GBP.
- **Local schema:** `LocalBusiness` (or the right subtype) with `address`,
  `geo`, `openingHours`, `telephone`, `priceRange`, `sameAs`.
- **Reviews:** volume, recency, rating, and owner responses on GBP. Reviews are
  a major local ranking and conversion factor.
- **Local landing pages:** for multi-location or multi-area businesses, a
  dedicated, indexable page per location/area with unique content (not
  doorway-page duplication).
- **Local links & citations:** presence in relevant local directories, chambers,
  and locally-relevant publications.
- **Map embed & directions:** correct map, embedded on the contact page.

## Reporting
Prioritise GBP completeness and NAP consistency first — they're high-impact and
usually low-effort. Tie each finding to the specific local query it unlocks
("bookstore in Kolkata", "[service] near me").
