# Research Frameworks Reference

Supporting reference for the `market-intelligence` skill.

---

## 1. Market Research Framework

### Market Sizing Approach (when TAM/SAM/SOM isn't the focus)
Even without full TAM/SAM/SOM sizing, always establish:
- **Market scale signal**: Is this a $10M, $100M, or $10B+ space? This determines ambition and competition intensity.
- **Growth direction**: Expanding, flat, or contracting? This determines urgency and investor appetite.
- **Maturity signal**: Are incumbents well-funded and entrenched, or is the market still being defined?

Useful proxies when exact sizing is unavailable:
- Funding rounds in the space (Crunchbase)
- Number of active competitors and their headcount (LinkedIn)
- Search volume trends (Google Trends)
- Industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC — look for press releases if paywalled)

### Growth Trajectory Signals to Search For
- YoY revenue growth of key players
- VC investment volume in the category (last 2 years)
- Job posting volume trends in the domain
- Conference/event growth in the space
- Media coverage frequency and sentiment shift

### Market Maturity Stages
| Stage | Signals | Implication for new entrant |
|---|---|---|
| **Emerging** | Few players, high VC activity, category still being named | High risk, high upside; educate the market |
| **Growing** | 5–15 players, expanding TAM, acquisition activity | Good window; differentiation is key |
| **Mature** | Dominated by 2–3 leaders, consolidation happening | Hard to displace; find underserved niches |
| **Declining** | Shrinking market, pivoting players, regulatory pressure | Avoid unless regulatory or tech shift creates reset |

## 2. Audience Psychographic Framework

### Segment Dimensions
| Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|---|---|
| **Demographics** | Age range, income bracket, education level, geography |
| **Role / Context** | Job title, industry, team size, decision-making authority |
| **Goals** | What are they trying to achieve? What does success look like? |
| **Frustrations** | What's broken in their current solution? What workarounds do they use? |
| **Triggers** | What event causes them to go looking for a solution? |
| **Trust signals** | What sources do they trust? Peers, analysts, media, certifications? |
| **Buying behaviour** | Self-serve or sales-led? Fast or deliberate decisions? Budget owner or influencer? |

### Jobs-to-Be-Done Framing
For each primary segment, map three job dimensions:
- **Functional job**: The practical task they're trying to accomplish
- **Emotional job**: How they want to feel while doing it / after doing it
- **Social job**: How they want to be perceived by others through using it

Example (project management tool):
- Functional: Track who's doing what and by when
- Emotional: Feel in control and not overwhelmed
- Social: Look like an organised, reliable leader to their team

### Where to Find Psychographic Data
- Reddit communities in the niche (r/[domain], look for complaints and workarounds)
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, App Store reviews (especially 3-star reviews — most honest)
- LinkedIn comments on thought leaders in the space
- Twitter/X conversations around competitor brand names
- Product Hunt comments on competitor launches
- YouTube comments on explainer/tutorial content in the category

## 3. Competitor Research Sources

### Tier 1 — Always Check
| Source | What to Find |
|---|---|
| Competitor website | Value prop, positioning, navigation = IA priorities, CTA language |
| Pricing page | Model, tiers, anchor pricing, what's gated |
| About/Team page | Founding story, team size signals, investor-facing vs user-facing narrative |
| Blog / Content hub | Topics they own, publishing cadence, SEO strategy |
| G2 / Capterra | Real user reviews, pros/cons, use cases, integration needs |
| LinkedIn company page | Headcount, hiring signals, recent announcements, audience engagement |

### Tier 2 — Go Deeper
| Source | What to Find |
|---|---|
| Crunchbase | Funding history, investor profile, acquisition history |
| SimilarWeb | Traffic volume, top channels, audience geography |
| App Store / Play Store | Ratings, review themes, update cadence |
| Reddit / Quora | Unprompted user sentiment, comparisons, frustrations |
| Twitter/X brand mentions | Real-time sentiment, customer service quality |
| Wayback Machine | How their positioning has evolved |
| Job postings | What they're investing in, team structure, tech stack |

### Tier 3 — If Time Allows
- Podcast appearances by founders (positioning language used with press)
- Conference talks (strategic direction signals)
- Patent filings (if deep tech)
- SEC filings or Glassdoor (if public or culture-relevant)

## 4. Brand Identity Analysis Framework

### Voice & Tone Dimensions
| Dimension | Spectrum | How to Identify |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | Formal ←→ Casual | Sentence length, contractions, jargon |
| Emotion | Clinical ←→ Warm | Adjectives, empathy language, humour |
| Authority | Deferential ←→ Assertive | Claim strength, hedging language |
| Complexity | Simple ←→ Expert | Technical vocabulary, assumed knowledge |
| Personality | Neutral ←→ Distinctive | Brand-specific vocabulary, recurring metaphors |

Look at: homepage hero copy, error messages, email subject lines, social captions, customer success language.

### Visual Identity Description (without screenshots)
Infer from:
- Website colour feel: vibrant vs muted, monochrome vs colourful, warm vs cool
- Typography signals: serif (traditional, trustworthy) vs sans-serif (modern, clean) vs display (expressive)
- Design density: minimal white space (calm, premium) vs content-dense (utility, value)
- Imagery style: photography vs illustration vs data visualisation vs abstract
- Motion/animation: present and playful vs static and serious

Use descriptive language a designer can act on:
❌ "They have a blue website"
✅ "Navy + electric teal palette — projects authority and tech-forward energy. Clean sans-serif (likely Inter or Helvetica). Generous whitespace. Hero uses abstract data visualisation rather than product screenshots — deliberately avoiding feature-first messaging."

### Messaging & Positioning Dimensions
- **Category claim**: What market category do they say they're in?
- **Primary audience signal**: Who do they speak to in their hero headline?
- **Core value prop**: What transformation do they promise?
- **Proof type**: Social proof via logos, stats, testimonials, awards, certifications?
- **Objection handling**: What fears do they preemptively address?
- **CTA language**: What action do they ask for first? (Book a demo = sales-led; Start free = product-led)

## 5. SWOT Analysis Framework

For each competitor, fill this with **specific, evidenced claims** — not generic observations.

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths**: What makes them hard to beat? Specific moats (network effects, integrations, brand, data, team) | **Weaknesses**: Specific gaps, complaints, limitations, pricing friction |
| **External** | **Opportunities**: Market moves they could make that would strengthen their position | **Threats**: What could undermine them? (Your product, regulation, tech shift, macro) |

**Quality bar for SWOT entries:**
❌ "Strength: Good product" → useless
✅ "Strength: 400+ native integrations create high switching cost — users are embedded in workflows" → actionable

## 6. Strategic Edge Analysis Framework

### Differentiation Durability Test
For each claimed differentiator, ask:
1. **Is it real?** Can you demonstrate it, not just claim it?
2. **Is it valued?** Does the target audience actually care about this?
3. **Is it rare?** Do competitors also do this, or not yet?
4. **Is it defensible?** How long until a competitor copies it? What makes it sticky?

Only differentiators that pass all four are genuine strategic edges.

### Feature Gap Matrix
```
Pain Point Severity (High/Med/Low) × Competitor Coverage (Full/Partial/None)

High severity + None/Partial coverage = 🔥 Primary opportunity
Med severity + None coverage = 🟡 Secondary opportunity  
High severity + Full coverage = ⚠️ Table stakes — must match, can't win here
Low severity + None coverage = 💡 Nice to have — not a launch priority
```

### Go-to-Market Positioning Formula
```
For [specific audience segment]
who struggle with [specific pain point],
[Product Name] is a [category frame]
that [primary benefit / outcome].

Unlike [primary competitor],
we [key differentiator that the competitor cannot or does not claim].
```

### Pricing Model Selection Guide
| Model | Best for | Signals it's right |
|---|---|---|
| **Freemium** | Product-led growth, viral loops, individual users | Competitors offer free; low CAC market; product sells itself |
| **Subscription tiers** | SaaS, recurring value, team/org usage | Clear usage segments; upsell path obvious |
| **Usage-based** | API, infra, consumption products | Value scales with usage; hard to predict seats |
| **One-time / Lifetime** | Tools, utilities, indie market | No ongoing service cost; community trust via fair pricing |
| **Enterprise / Custom** | High ACV, complex orgs, compliance needs | Sales-led motion; budget exists; long sales cycles |
| **Hybrid** | Freemium → team → enterprise | Clear expansion path from individual to org |
